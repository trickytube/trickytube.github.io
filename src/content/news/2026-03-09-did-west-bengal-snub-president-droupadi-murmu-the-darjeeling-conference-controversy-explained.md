### Did West Bengal Disrespect the President? Inside the Darjeeling Santhal Conference Controversy

**What happens when the First Citizen of India arrives at an official event—and the state’s Chief Minister is nowhere to receive her?**

That question triggered a heated political controversy recently during the 9th International Santhal Conference in Darjeeling, where President Droupadi Murmu’s visit became the center of a national debate. What should have been a cultural celebration for the Santhal tribal community instead turned into a political flashpoint involving the West Bengal government, the BJP, and questions about protocol and respect for constitutional offices.

Let’s break down what really happened and why it sparked such strong reactions across the political spectrum.

### A Conference Meant to Celebrate Tribal Heritage

The International Santhal Conference is an important cultural event for the Santhal community, one of India’s largest tribal groups. It aims to celebrate Santhal culture, identity, and socio-political issues affecting tribal populations.

Given the significance of the event, the presence of President Droupadi Murmu—India’s first tribal President—was symbolically powerful. Her participation was expected to elevate the event’s importance and bring national attention to tribal issues.

However, the situation took an unexpected turn even before the conference began.

### The Venue Change That Triggered Frustration

Initially, the conference was planned at a large open ground in Siliguri, a location considered accessible and capable of accommodating thousands of attendees from across the region.

But the venue was suddenly shifted to Gosainpur in Darjeeling, a smaller location that reportedly had limited capacity and accessibility.

This change frustrated many members of the Santhal community. According to reports, the smaller venue led to lower attendance, with many people unable to participate in the event despite traveling long distances.

For a community gathering meant to highlight tribal pride and unity, the change felt disappointing to many attendees. Some critics argued that such an important cultural event deserved better planning and broader participation.

### The Protocol Lapse Allegation

The controversy escalated further due to allegations of a protocol lapse involving President Murmu’s arrival.

Under established government protocol in India, when the President visits a state, the Chief Minister is generally expected to receive the President at the airport or formally welcome them.

Reports claimed that West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee did not personally receive President Murmu when she arrived for the conference. Instead, local officials and representatives reportedly handled the reception.

For critics, this was seen as more than just a scheduling issue—it was framed as a lack of respect for the constitutional office of the President.

Given that President Murmu herself comes from a tribal background, the optics of the situation quickly became politically sensitive.

### BJP Leaders React Strongly

Soon after the reports emerged, Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Home Minister Amit Shah publicly criticized the West Bengal government.

Their statements framed the incident as a disrespect not only toward the President but also toward India’s tribal communities.

Supporters of the BJP argued that the situation reflected political indifference toward tribal representation, especially considering that President Murmu’s rise to the presidency had been widely celebrated as a milestone for tribal empowerment.

Some political commentators also suggested that the controversy could influence future political narratives around tribal outreach, particularly in eastern India where tribal populations hold significant electoral influence.

### TMC’s Defense: Security and Scheduling Issues

The Trinamool Congress (TMC), led by Mamata Banerjee, quickly pushed back against the allegations.

Party leaders stated that the venue change was made due to security considerations, not political motives. According to them, authorities determined that the original venue in Siliguri posed logistical and security challenges.

Regarding the protocol issue, TMC representatives argued that appropriate government officials were present to receive the President, and that such arrangements are sometimes handled by designated representatives rather than the Chief Minister personally.

They also accused the BJP of politicizing the event for electoral gain, claiming the controversy was being amplified to attack the West Bengal government.

### Why This Controversy Matters

At first glance, the issue may appear to be a routine political dispute. But the deeper implications go beyond protocol.

First, President Droupadi Murmu represents a historic milestone for India’s tribal communities. Any controversy surrounding her treatment is bound to draw attention because it touches on issues of representation, respect, and symbolism.

Second, the debate reflects the ongoing political rivalry between the BJP and the TMC, particularly in West Bengal where both parties are competing to expand their influence.

In my view, this controversy also highlights a broader challenge in Indian politics: events meant to celebrate culture and community often become battlegrounds for political narratives.

The Santhal Conference was supposed to focus on tribal heritage. Instead, the headlines were dominated by political accusations.

### The Bigger Picture

Whether the incident was truly a protocol lapse or simply a misinterpreted logistical decision remains debated. But one thing is clear—the controversy has once again shown how symbolism and protocol can quickly escalate into national political debates in India.

For the Santhal community, the hope now is that future events will bring the focus back to what truly matters: celebrating culture, strengthening community identity, and addressing the real challenges faced by tribal populations.
